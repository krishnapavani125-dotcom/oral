Awareness

Know the Risk Factors

Understanding risk factors is the first step toward prevention. Oral cancer is largely preventable with early awareness and lifestyle changes.

🚬

Tobacco Use

Smoking and smokeless tobacco (gutka, betel quid) are leading causes of oral cancer. Cessation significantly reduces risk.

🍶

Alcohol Consumption

Heavy and prolonged alcohol use increases oral cancer risk, especially when combined with tobacco use.

🦠

HPV Infection

Human Papillomavirus (HPV-16) is strongly linked to oropharyngeal cancers, especially in younger adults.

☀️

Sun Exposure

Chronic UV exposure to lips is a known risk for lip cancer. SPF lip balm and hats provide effective protection.

🧬

Genetic Predisposition

A family history of oral or head-and-neck cancers can increase susceptibility. Genetic counselling can help assess personal risk.

🪥

Poor Oral Hygiene

Chronic irritation from ill-fitting dentures or persistent poor oral hygiene can promote the development of cancerous lesions over time.
